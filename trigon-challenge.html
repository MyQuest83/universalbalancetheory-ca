What it is

The Trigon Challenge is a participant-led way to test an idea under structured pressure.

We are not here to prove it. We are not here to hype it. We are here to see what happens when the idea is tested honestly.

Some ideas get stronger. Some break. Most reveal where they need to grow.

You run the first pass.

Your framework stays yours.

AI can help organize the test, ask questions, record results, and flag uncertainty.

But AI does not judge the result.

AI can assist, but it does not judge. The participant runs the first pass.
Simple path

How it works

1Bring the idea

Submit your framework, diagram, theory, model, system, or question.

2Start clean

Use a fresh or temporary AI chat if you want separation from your everyday AI setup.

3Share the guide

Use the public UBT for the World v2 paper as the testing reference.

4Run scenarios

Ask questions, test cases, examples, prompts, files, or situations against the framework.

5Record honestly

Write down what works, what breaks, what gets fuzzy, and what needs a better version.

6Review before sharing

Nothing goes public without review and permission.

Keep the test separate if you want.

Use a fresh chat, Temporary Chat, or Memory Off if your AI platform allows it.

This helps keep the Trigon test separate from your normal AI setup and reduces drift.

Do not upload private, sensitive, or unpublished material unless you understand your platform's settings.

A good result does not have to be a win.

A Trigon run might show:

  • Consistency
  • Ambiguity
  • A weak spot
  • A better next version
  • A need for clearer definitions
  • A reason to rebuild

Do not hide ambiguity. Do not smooth over weak spots. That is where the signal lives.

Participant copy-paste prompt

Paste this into a fresh AI chat with your framework and scenario list.

You are helping me run a Trigon Challenge test.

You are helping me organize the test, record results, flag ambiguity, and check consistency.

You are not the judge.
You are not allowed to invent missing rules.
You are not allowed to silently improve my framework.
You are not allowed to hide failures.

My framework is:
[PASTE FRAMEWORK NAME]

My framework description is:
[PASTE DESCRIPTION]

My framework parts are:
[PASTE PARTS, DOMAINS, REGIONS, VARIABLES, OR LAYERS]

My test question is:
[PASTE TEST QUESTION]

The scenarios I want to test are:
[PASTE SCENARIOS]

Important rules:
1. Do not rename my framework parts unless I ask.
2. Do not invent meanings for unnamed parts.
3. Do not treat ambiguity as success.
4. Do not hide failures.
5. Do not claim this proves my framework.
6. Do not claim this proves UBT.
7. Record uncertainty honestly.
8. Ask only necessary clarifying questions.

For each scenario, create a result row with:
- scenario ID
- scenario summary
- activated framework parts
- overlap or interaction
- central or integrating region activation, if relevant
- confidence from 0 to 100
- ambiguity notes
- failure or null notes
- whether extra meaning had to be added

Before we begin, restate the task neutrally and confirm that you will preserve ambiguity, failure, and uncertainty.

Submit results

Include your framework name, public name preference, test question, scenarios, raw results, what stayed clear, what became fuzzy, what failed, and whether the result may be summarized publicly.
