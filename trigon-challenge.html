Three depths

Choose how far the test should go.

Light, Medium, and Heavy describe the depth of the run. They do not rank the importance of the idea.

△Light TestA clean first pass for clarity, overlap, and obvious failure points.
  • Scan the public archive.
  • Define the question and framework parts.
  • Run a small scenario set.
  • Record ambiguity, nulls, and AI-added meaning.
Use Light Depth
△Medium TestA structured run with sources, controls, cross-checks, and reusable data.
  • Complete the archive scan and preflight.
  • Lock variables, sources, and conditions.
  • Add baseline, null, and negative controls.
  • Prepare downloadable data when permitted.
Use Medium Depth
△Heavy TestA full research lane for preregistration, data lock, stress tests, and reproducibility.
  • Preregister hypotheses and failure criteria.
  • Freeze data and analysis choices.
  • Run robustness and replication checks.
  • Prepare code, data, checksums, and Record-P.
Use Heavy Depth

What the Trigon Challenge is

The Trigon Challenge is a participant-led way to test an idea under structured pressure.

The goal is to see what happens when the idea is tested honestly. Some ideas get stronger. Some break. Most reveal where they need to grow.

AI can assist, but it does not judge. The participant runs the first pass.
Simple path

How it works

1Bring the idea

Submit your framework, diagram, theory, model, system, or question.

2Scan first

Search the Trigon Ledger for related records, prior failures, and reusable sources.

3Start clean

Use a fresh or temporary AI chat when you want separation from your everyday setup.

4Share the guide

Use the released UBT for the World v2 paper as the public testing reference.

5Run and record

Test scenarios and preserve what works, breaks, stays unclear, or requires added meaning.

6Review before sharing

Nothing becomes public without review, permission, and a clear release status.

Keep the test separate when needed.

Use a fresh chat, Temporary Chat, or Memory Off if your AI platform allows it. This can reduce drift and keep the test separate from your normal AI setup.

Do not upload private, sensitive, or unpublished material unless you understand the platform settings and release implications.

A good result does not have to be a win.

A Trigon run might show:

  • Consistency
  • Ambiguity
  • A weak spot
  • A better next version
  • A need for clearer definitions
  • A reason to rebuild

Do not hide ambiguity or smooth over weak spots. That is where the signal lives.

Participant copy-paste prompt

Paste this into a fresh AI chat with your framework and scenario list.

You are helping me run a Trigon Challenge test.

You are helping me organize the test, record results, flag ambiguity, and check consistency.

You are not the judge.
You are not allowed to invent missing rules.
You are not allowed to silently improve my framework.
You are not allowed to hide failures.

My testing lane is:
[PASTE LIFE, NATURE / SCIENCE, OR TECH]

My test depth is:
[PASTE LIGHT, MEDIUM, OR HEAVY]

My framework is:
[PASTE FRAMEWORK NAME]

My framework description is:
[PASTE DESCRIPTION]

My framework parts are:
[PASTE PARTS, DOMAINS, REGIONS, VARIABLES, OR LAYERS]

My test question is:
[PASTE TEST QUESTION]

The scenarios I want to test are:
[PASTE SCENARIOS]

Important rules:
1. Do not rename my framework parts unless I ask.
2. Do not invent meanings for unnamed parts.
3. Do not treat ambiguity as success.
4. Do not hide failures.
5. Do not claim this proves my framework.
6. Do not claim this proves UBT.
7. Record uncertainty honestly.
8. Ask only necessary clarifying questions.

For each scenario, create a result row with:
- scenario ID
- scenario summary
- activated framework parts
- overlap or interaction
- central or integrating region activation, if relevant
- confidence from 0 to 100
- ambiguity notes
- failure or null notes
- whether extra meaning had to be added

Before we begin, restate the task neutrally and confirm that you will preserve ambiguity, failure, and uncertainty.
