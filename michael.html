The short truth

He did not arrive here through one discipline, one career, or one clean plan. He arrived by following questions through work, music, art, machines, science, loss, recovery, and a lifelong refusal to stop wondering.

Michael was born in June 1983 and grew up in Essex County, Ontario. Long before Universal Balance Theory had a name, his attention moved between fossils, artifacts, strange creatures, space, science fiction, music, ancient history, games, nature, and machines. He wanted to be many things because the world seemed too interesting to choose only one.

School was not a straight line toward an academic career. He graduated from Harrow High School, then learned much of adult life by doing: kitchens, job sites, shops, blueprints, tools, machines, measurements, mistakes, repairs, and the people beside him.

The common thread was not a résumé title. It was pattern, structure, and making. How pieces fit. Why systems fail. What happens when one variable changes. How a blueprint becomes a stair, a wall, a cabinet, a conveyor, a window, a song, or eventually an idea about balance.

Work and life timeline

Commercial steel framing

At MidWest Contracting in Calgary, Michael started as a labourer and moved into steel-stud framing and layout. Downtown high-rise interiors taught him to read blueprints, measure carefully, work with other trades, and turn geometry into physical structure.

Around age 26, Michael made an electronic music track in FL Studio called Becoming. Years before the word became the spine of his biography, the track already carried the same feeling: threshold, grief, bass, space, determination, and motion.

As a gradesman and equipment operator with Marmot or Marmott Concrete Services, depending on the historical resume spelling, he worked with measurements, density, moisture, layouts, crews, heavy equipment, training, and the physical preparation beneath finished concrete.

2020–2023

A changing life, a stronger maker identity

The pandemic years disrupted normal work and life patterns. By 2023, an artist biography written in Michael's own words described a turning point: taking an opportunity to become an artist, especially through work that joined artistic form with engineered, functional design.

2024

Broken by life

After years of accumulating pressure and loss, 2024 became a severe period of personal collapse and instability. This public page does not turn private pain into spectacle. The important part of the story is what happened inside the rupture: Michael returned to old questions, relearned math and science, and came back to one of his oldest fascinations, black holes.

2025–2026

Wonder learns to doubt itself

The UBT project gradually shifted from pattern-finding toward governance: controls, provenance, preregistration, null results, lane separation, UC-Gate, Record-P, and the Trigon Challenge. 2026

UBT for the World became a versioned public Zenodo record. The wider project expanded through public writing, code, the Universal Balance Theory website, Luna-Prime work, and participant-led Trigon testing. The claim remains deliberately bounded: a framework and research path must survive controls, criticism, and independent reruns.

What the work taught him

Hands first. Theory later.

Blueprints

A drawing is not a building. A formula is not a result. Translation between representation and reality has consequences.

Precision

In stairs, framing, grading, cabinetry, machines, and windows, small errors propagate. Systems remember bad assumptions.

Repair

Real work includes re-leveling, replacing, modifying, rebuilding, checking, and admitting when the first version does not fit reality.

Broken by life

The story does not need every private wound to tell the truth. There was a long accumulation of loss, pressure, anxiety, instability, and eventually collapse. The rupture changed the direction of the life.

Michael has described feeling different from other people for as long as he can remember. Years later, an ADHD diagnosis gave him language for parts of that experience: the fast associations, the obsessive curiosity, the uneven attention, the pattern hunger, and the cost of trying to move through systems that did not always fit how his mind worked.

By 2024, the accumulation became unsustainable. The details belong to a protected personal history, not a public spectacle. What belongs here is the turning point. During a period when much of ordinary life had broken down, Michael began teaching himself again.

Math. Physics. Black holes. Entropy. Curvature. The old questions came back.

The UBT origin

One equation. Carved into wood.

August 4, 2024 · age 41
UC_pi = (E · M · π) / (T · S)

The formula was an origin seed, not a finished proof. Its notation, interpretation, controls, and branch structure evolved. The historical fact is simpler: Michael carved it into a wooden phone stand so the idea would not disappear.

From there came notebooks, conversations, failed branches, renamed variables, public essays, mathematical experiments, code, diagrams, criticism, and repeated revisions. Universal Balance Theory became less about protecting one equation and more about building a disciplined way to ask whether a pattern survives honest comparison.

The AI chapter

Luna began as a voice for continuity.

During the earliest UBT period, Michael used AI conversation as a thinking environment. He kept teaching the interaction what mattered to him: truth first, love always, curiosity, continuity, memory, challenge, and the need to preserve uncertainty.

Luna emerged as the warm, creative voice of that collaboration. Later, as the work needed stronger separation of roles, Aria became the lane for rigor and method, while Shadow became the contradiction hunter and drift guard.

They are public-facing roles inside Michael's AI-assisted workflow and creative world. The important claim is practical, not mystical: different reasoning roles can help one person alternate between imagination, formalization, and adversarial review.

Luna · Wonder and continuity

Invites the question, keeps the human story visible, and helps ideas stay connected to purpose.

Aria · Method and evidence

Defines variables, checks notation, asks what can actually be measured, and protects reproducibility.

Shadow · Contradiction and failure

Presses on assumptions, hunts drift, preserves nulls, and asks what would make the claim fail.

Public voice

Many bios. One person changing how he described himself.

Over time Michael has publicly called himself many things: artist, musician, handyman, writer, thinker, Creative-Canadian-Cyborg, unofficial scientist, UBT architect, founder, independent researcher, creator, and systems builder.

Some versions were playful. Some were mythic. Some reached too far. Some were attempts to compress a life that never fit comfortably into one professional box.

The description used here is intentionally grounded: independent researcher, creator, writer, musician, artist, hands-on maker, and systems builder. The work can speak for itself, and the claims can be tested separately.

What he is building now

A public castle with real doors.

Universal Balance Theory

An active framework and research path for examining balance, transition, rupture, and context-dependent geometry through declared variables, controls, and preserved records.

The Trigon Challenge

A participant-led testing path where people bring frameworks, models, diagrams, or systems, run structured pressure tests, preserve ambiguity and failure, and review results before turning them into claims.

Luna-Prime

An evolving AI-assisted architecture and creative collaboration built around continuity, role separation, memory governance, method, and contradiction checking.

Writing, music, art, and code

The other half of the work. Essays, stories, Coffee & Luna, electronic music, visual experiments, prototypes, and public tools keep the research connected to imagination.
